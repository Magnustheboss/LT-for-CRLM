Patient Selection Criteria for LT in CRLM

Overview

The success of LT for CRLM depends critically on rigorous patient selection. Multiple scoring systems and prognostic factors have been developed to identify patients with favorable tumor biology who will benefit most from transplantation.

The Oslo Score

The Oslo Score was developed from the SECA-I trial and is one of the most widely used tools for predicting post-LT survival in CRLM patients.

Oslo Score Components (1 point each)

1. Largest Tumor Size ≥ 5.5 cm

Larger tumors indicate greater disease burden and are associated with worse outcomes.

2. Progressive Disease on Chemotherapy

Disease progression despite systemic therapy indicates aggressive biology.

3. Plasma CEA Level > 80 µg/L

Elevated CEA suggests higher tumor burden and poorer prognosis.

4. Time from Primary Tumor Resection to LT < 2 Years

Short interval suggests rapid metastatic progression.

Oslo Score Interpretation

Oslo Score 5-Year OS 10-Year OS Risk Category
0-1 63.4% - 92.0% 45.7% - 88.9% Favorable
2 ~60% ~45% Intermediate
3-4 8.3% 0% Unfavorable
Clinical Note: An Oslo Score of 0-2 is generally considered acceptable for LT consideration, while scores of 3-4 are associated with poor outcomes and should generally be excluded.

Fong Clinical Risk Score (FCRS)

The FCRS was originally developed for liver resection but has been validated for LT in CRLM patients.

FCRS Components (1 point each)

1. Node-Positive Primary Tumor
2. Disease-Free Interval < 12 Months
3. More Than 1 Liver Tumor
4. Preoperative CEA > 200 ng/mL
5. Size of Largest Tumor > 5 cm

FCRS Interpretation

FCRS 5-Year OS Risk Category
0-2 75% - 80% Favorable
≥ 3 40% Unfavorable

Metabolic Tumor Volume (PET-MTV)

18F-FDG PET/CT is essential for excluding extrahepatic disease and quantifying disease burden. The metabolic tumor volume (MTV) is a powerful prognostic factor.

PET-MTV Prognostic Value

PET-MTV 3-Year OS 5-Year OS
< 70 cm³ 90.9% 63.6%
≥ 70 cm³ 50% 25%

Clinical Application: PET-MTV < 70 cm³ is associated with significantly improved outcomes and should be used as a selection criterion.

Molecular Prognostic Factors

Recent advances in molecular profiling have identified specific genetic alterations that predict response to LT and post-transplant survival.

RAS/TP53 Co-mutations

Negative Prognostic Impact: Co-mutations of RAS and TP53 are associated with significantly worse outcomes:

  • Median OS with RAS/TP53 co-mutations: 21 months
  • Median OS without co-mutations: 60 months
  • Hazard Ratio: 4.0 (95% CI 1.7-9.3)

Recommendation: Patients with RAS/TP53 co-mutations should be carefully counseled and may be better served by alternative treatment strategies.

BRAF V600E Mutation

Exclusion Criterion: BRAF V600E mutations are associated with poor prognosis and aggressive disease. Patients with this mutation should be excluded from LT consideration per IHPBA consensus guidelines.

Microsatellite Instability and Mismatch Repair Status

IHPBA guidelines recommend that patients must have microsatellite stability and be mismatch repair proficient to be considered for LT.

IHPBA Consensus Exclusion Criteria

The International Hepato-Pancreato-Biliary Association has established clear exclusion criteria for LT in CRLM:

Pathological Exclusions

  • Undifferentiated adenocarcinoma histology
  • Signet ring cell carcinoma
  • Extensive nodal disease (N2 or greater)
  • BRAF V600E mutation
  • Microsatellite instability or mismatch repair deficiency

Radiological/Clinical Exclusions

  • Extrahepatic metastases (except prior resected pulmonary metastases)
  • Local recurrence of primary tumor
  • Inadequate response to bridging chemotherapy

General Transplant Exclusions

  • Active infection or uncontrolled sepsis
  • Significant comorbidities precluding surgery
  • Poor performance status (ECOG > 1)
  • Active substance abuse

Recommended Evaluation Protocol

Pre-Transplant Workup

  1. Imaging: Contrast-enhanced CT or MRI of liver + 18F-FDG PET/CT
  2. Tumor Markers: CEA level
  3. Molecular Testing: KRAS, NRAS, BRAF, TP53 mutations; microsatellite instability status
  4. Chemotherapy Response: Assess response to ≥3 months of systemic therapy
  5. Scoring: Calculate Oslo Score, FCRS, and PET-MTV
  6. Multidisciplinary Review: Oncology, surgery, transplant, and radiology consensus
