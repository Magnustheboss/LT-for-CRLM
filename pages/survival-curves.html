Interactive Kaplan-Meier Survival Curves

Evidence-based survival outcomes from major clinical trials

About These Curves

These interactive Kaplan-Meier curves display survival outcomes from landmark clinical trials in liver transplantation for colorectal liver metastases. Use these curves to:

  • Compare survival by risk stratification (Oslo Score)
  • Evaluate long-term outcomes after LT
  • Compare LT vs. chemotherapy outcomes
  • Assess disease-free survival patterns
  • Present data to patients and families

Overall Survival by Oslo Score

Data Source: Dueland et al. (2023) - Long-Term Survival, Prognostic Factors, and Selection of Patients With Colorectal Cancer for Liver Transplant

Clinical Significance: The Oslo Score is the most powerful prognostic factor for LT in CRLM, with dramatic differences in survival between risk groups.

Key Findings:

  • Oslo Score 0-1 (Favorable): 88.9% 10-year OS - Excellent candidates for LT
  • Oslo Score 2 (Intermediate): 55.2% 10-year OS - Consider LT with careful evaluation
  • Oslo Score 3-4 (Unfavorable): 11.7% 10-year OS - Poor LT candidates, consider chemotherapy
  • Hazard Ratio: 7.6× difference between best and worst groups

Overall Survival: LT vs. Chemotherapy Alone

Data Source: Adam et al. (2024) - TransMet Trial - Liver transplantation plus chemotherapy versus chemotherapy alone

Clinical Significance: First randomized controlled trial showing survival benefit of LT for permanently unresectable CRLM.

Key Findings:

  • LT + Chemotherapy: 3-year OS 55% - Significant survival advantage
  • Chemotherapy Alone: 3-year OS 27% - Standard palliative approach
  • Median OS: LT group not reached vs. 41 months (chemo)
  • Hazard Ratio: 0.48 (52% reduction in mortality risk)
  • P-value: 0.027 (statistically significant)

Disease-Free Survival After LT

Data Source: Multiple trials (SECA-I/II, TransMet) - Disease-free survival patterns

Clinical Significance: Understanding recurrence patterns helps manage expectations and post-LT surveillance.

Key Findings:

  • 1-year DFS: 35-40% - Most recurrences occur in first year
  • 3-year DFS: 20-25% - Plateau after 3 years
  • Recurrence Pattern: Predominantly extrahepatic (lungs, peritoneum)
  • Median OS After Recurrence: 37.1 months - Indolent disease course
  • Clinical Pearl: Recurrence is not failure; many patients have long survival

Overall Survival by PET-MTV

Data Source: Moosavi et al. (2025) - Molecular prognostic factors for LT in unresectable CRLM

Clinical Significance: Metabolic tumor volume is an independent prognostic factor complementary to Oslo Score.

Key Findings:

  • PET-MTV < 70 cm³: Superior survival outcomes
  • PET-MTV ≥ 70 cm³: Significantly worse outcomes
  • Hazard Ratio: 2.5× difference between groups
  • Clinical Use: Integrate with Oslo Score for best risk stratification
  • Advantage: Captures biological aggressiveness of tumor

Clinical Interpretation Guide

How to Use These Curves

For Patient Counseling:

  • Identify patient's Oslo Score and locate on first curve
  • Discuss realistic survival expectations
  • Explain that recurrence doesn't mean failure
  • Emphasize importance of post-LT surveillance

For Clinical Decision-Making:

  • Oslo Score 0-1: Strong LT candidate - proceed with evaluation
  • Oslo Score 2: Intermediate - discuss risks/benefits carefully
  • Oslo Score 3-4: Poor candidate - consider chemotherapy
  • Combine with PET-MTV for additional prognostic information
  • Consider molecular factors (RAS/TP53) for borderline cases

Important Caveats:

  • These are population-based curves; individual outcomes vary
  • Curves based on selected populations meeting strict criteria
  • Outcomes depend on institutional expertise and patient factors
  • Always discuss with multidisciplinary team
  • Shared decision-making is essential
