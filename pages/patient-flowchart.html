Interactive Patient Selection Flowchart

Step-by-step guide for evaluating candidates for liver transplantation in CRLM

How to Use This Flowchart

This interactive flowchart guides you through the systematic evaluation of patients with colorectal liver metastases for liver transplantation. Answer each question to determine if the patient is a suitable candidate.

Important: This tool is for educational purposes and should not replace clinical judgment. Always consult with your multidisciplinary transplant team.

Final Assessment

Multidisciplinary Team Discussion

✓ Resectable Disease

This patient is a candidate for hepatic resection. LT is not indicated for resectable disease.

Recommendation: Proceed with surgical resection by experienced hepatobiliary surgeon.

✗ Extrahepatic Metastases

Presence of extrahepatic disease is a relative contraindication to LT.

Recommendation: Consider chemotherapy or locoregional therapy. Discuss with transplant team if disease can be controlled.

✗ Exclusion Criteria Present

This patient does not meet criteria for liver transplantation.

Recommendation: Continue palliative chemotherapy. Reassess if exclusion criteria are resolved.

✓ Potential Candidate for LT

Next Steps

If the patient appears to be a potential candidate, proceed with:

  1. Comprehensive multidisciplinary team evaluation
  2. Detailed imaging assessment (CT, MRI, PET-CT)
  3. Functional assessment and comorbidity evaluation
  4. Psychosocial evaluation
  5. Formal transplant candidacy assessment
