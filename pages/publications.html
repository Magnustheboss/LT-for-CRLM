Research Group Publications

Key publications from the Transplant Oncology Research Group, Oslo University Hospital

Featured Publications on LT for CRLM

The Transplant Oncology Research Group at Oslo University Hospital has published extensively on liver transplantation for colorectal liver metastases. Below are key publications that have shaped the evidence base for this emerging treatment modality.

2024

Liver transplantation plus chemotherapy versus chemotherapy alone in patients with permanently unresectable colorectal liver metastases (TransMet): results from a multicentre, open-label, prospective, randomised controlled trial

Adam R, Dueland S, Smedman TM, et al.

The Lancet, 2024

Significance: The first randomized controlled trial demonstrating that liver transplantation plus chemotherapy significantly improves 5-year overall survival (56.6% vs 12.6%) compared to chemotherapy alone in patients with permanently unresectable CRLM.

Key Finding: Hazard ratio 0.37 (95% CI 0.22-0.62), representing a 4.5-fold survival benefit for LT.

Resection Details, Donor Segment Volume, and Adjuvant Chemotherapy After Living Donor Liver Transplantation for Colorectal Liver Metastases

Dueland S, Grut H, Solheim JM, et al.

Transplantation, 2024

Significance: Comprehensive analysis of living donor liver transplantation (LDLT) outcomes for CRLM, addressing technical aspects and adjuvant therapy.

Key Finding: LDLT expands access to transplantation and offers comparable or superior outcomes compared to deceased donor LT.

2023

Long-Term Survival, Prognostic Factors, and Selection of Patients With Colorectal Cancer for Liver Transplant: A Nonrandomized Controlled Trial

Dueland S, Smedman TM, Grut H, et al.

JAMA Surgery, 2023

Cited by 82

Significance: Landmark study establishing the Oslo Score and demonstrating long-term survival outcomes in carefully selected CRLM patients undergoing LT.

Key Findings:

  • 10-year overall survival: 88.9% (Oslo Score 0-1) vs 11.7% (Oslo Score 3-4)
  • Oslo Score is a validated prognostic tool for patient selection
  • Molecular profiling (RAS/TP53 status) predicts outcomes

Transplantation for Nonresectable Colorectal Liver Metastases: Long-Term Follow-Up of the First Prospective Pilot Study

Solheim JM, Dueland S, Line PD, et al.

Annals of Surgery, 2023

Cited by 65

Significance: Long-term follow-up of the pioneering SECA-I trial, confirming the durability of LT outcomes for CRLM.

Key Finding: LT for carefully selected nrCRLM can provide long-term survival and potentially cure for selected patients, with OS excellent compared to oncological treatment alone.

Where Are We Now With Liver Transplant for Colorectal Metastasis?

Ch√°vez-Villa C, Dueland S, Smedman TM, et al.

Current Transplantation Reports, 2023

Significance: Comprehensive review of the current state of LT for CRLM, summarizing evidence and clinical practice.

Key Focus: Patient selection criteria, prognostic factors, and outcomes in the modern era.

2022

Survival After Liver Resection and Liver Transplantation for Colorectal Liver Metastases: A Comparative Analysis Stratified by Metabolic Tumor Volume Assessed by PET

Grut H, Line PD, Labori KJ, Schulz A, Dueland S

HPB (Oxford), 2022

Cited by 8

Significance: Comparative effectiveness study demonstrating that LT provides superior outcomes compared to resection in patients with high metabolic tumor volume.

Key Finding: PET-MTV is a valuable prognostic factor for stratifying patients and selecting optimal treatment modality.

2021

Liver Transplantation for Non-Resectable Colorectal Liver Metastases: The International Hepato-Pancreato-Biliary Association Consensus Guidelines

Bonney GK, Dueland S, Smedman TM, et al.

The Lancet Gastroenterology & Hepatology, 2021

Significance: International consensus guidelines establishing evidence-based recommendations for LT in CRLM.

Key Contributions: Selection criteria, patient evaluation protocols, and best practices for multidisciplinary assessment.

Survival Outcomes After Portal Vein Embolization and Liver Resection Compared With Liver Transplant for Patients With Extensive Colorectal Cancer Liver Metastases

Dueland S, Grut H, Smedman TM, et al.

JAMA Surgery, 2021

Significance: Comparative effectiveness study showing superior outcomes with LT versus PVE + resection for extensive CRLM.

Key Finding: 5-year OS 33.4% (LT) vs 6.7% (PVE + resection) in high tumor load patients.

Liver Transplantation for Unresectable Colorectal Liver Metastases in Patients and Donors With Extended Criteria (SECA-II Arm D Study)

Smedman TM, Dueland S, Line PD, et al.

BJSOpen, 2020

Cited by 92

Significance: Landmark SECA-II trial demonstrating feasibility and safety of LT using extended criteria for both patients and donors.

Key Finding: Extended criteria can expand access to LT while maintaining acceptable outcomes in carefully selected patients.

2020 and Earlier

Liver Transplantation for Unresectable Colorectal Liver Metastases

Smedman TM, Hagness M, Dueland S, et al.

Annals of Surgery, 2019

Cited by 92

Significance: Pioneering work establishing the feasibility and efficacy of LT for CRLM in the modern era.

Key Finding: LT provides excellent long-term survival outcomes in carefully selected patients with unresectable CRLM.

Research Impact

The Transplant Oncology Research Group's publications have been instrumental in:

  • Establishing LT as a validated treatment option for unresectable CRLM
  • Developing evidence-based patient selection criteria (Oslo Score, FCRS, PET-MTV)
  • Demonstrating superior outcomes with LT compared to chemotherapy alone (TransMet trial)
  • Expanding access through living donor liver transplantation (LDLT)
  • Contributing to international consensus guidelines (IHPBA)
  • Advancing understanding of tumor biology and prognostic factors
