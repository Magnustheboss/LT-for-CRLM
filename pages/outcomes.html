The TransMet Trial: Definitive Evidence

The TransMet trial is the first multicenter, randomized controlled trial comparing LT plus chemotherapy to chemotherapy alone:

Study Design

  • 20 European tertiary centers
  • 94 patients randomized (47 LT + Chemo, 47 Chemo alone)
  • Median follow-up: 59.3 months
  • Inclusion: Permanently unresectable, liver-only CRLM; BRAF-non-mutated; responsive to chemotherapy

Primary Endpoint Results

Disease-Free Survival and Recurrence Patterns

One of the most important findings in LT for CRLM is the discrepancy between disease-free survival (DFS) and overall survival (OS):

The Recurrence Paradox

  • Median DFS: 11.8 months (Oslo cohort)
  • 5-Year DFS: 18.3% (Oslo cohort)
  • Recurrence Rate: 78.3% of patients
  • Median Time to Recurrence: 9.0 months
  • 94% of recurrences: Within 2 years of LT

Survival After Recurrence

Despite the high recurrence rate, survival after relapse is remarkably good:

  • Median OS from time of relapse: 37.1 months
  • 5-Year survival after relapse: 34.8%
  • 14 of 47 patients (29.8%): Alive >5 years after diagnosis of recurrence
  • One patient: Alive 156 months (13 years) after relapse
Clinical Significance: The indolent pattern of recurrence suggests that total hepatectomy effectively removes the primary tumor burden, while residual disease exhibits slower growth kinetics. This is distinct from the aggressive nature of CRLM in non-transplant patients.

Comparative Analysis: LT vs. Alternative Approaches

LT vs. Portal Vein Embolization + Liver Resection

For patients with high tumor load (HTL), LT offers superior outcomes compared to PVE followed by liver resection:

Treatment HTL Patients 5-Year OS
LT (all HTL) 29 patients 33.4%
PVE + LR (all HTL) 15 patients 19.2%
LT (HTL + Left-sided) Subset 45.3%
PVE + LR (HTL + Left-sided) Subset 12.5%

Key Finding: For patients with extensive disease and left-sided primary tumors, LT provides a 3.6-fold improvement in 5-year OS compared to PVE and resection.

Living Donor Liver Transplantation (LDLT)

LDLT offers an alternative to deceased donor LT and may provide superior outcomes:

North American LDLT Experience

  • First 10 LDLT cases: 1.5-year OS of 100%, DFS of 62%
  • US cohort (56.5% LDLT): 3-year OS 71.4% (LDLT) vs. 51.4% (DDLT)
  • Advantages: Shorter cold ischemia time, better graft quality, shorter waitlist time
  • Donor safety: Acceptable perioperative morbidity in living donors

Implication: LDLT circumvents deceased donor scarcity and may be the preferred approach in centers with experience in living donor transplantation.

Serious Adverse Events

The safety profile of LT for CRLM is important to consider:

Complication Incidence Notes
Serious Adverse Events (LT) 80% (32/40 patients) Comparable to other LT indications
Serious Adverse Events (Chemo) 83% (45/54 patients) Chemotherapy-related toxicity
Retransplantation 3 cases (TransMet) One perioperative death

Interpretation: The safety profile of LT is comparable to chemotherapy alone, with serious adverse events occurring in both groups at similar rates.

Summary of Key Outcomes

  • LT + Chemotherapy vs. Chemotherapy Alone: 56.6% vs. 12.6% 5-year OS (HR 0.37, p=0.0003)
  • Best Outcomes: Oslo Score 0-1 patients achieve 10-year OS of 88.9%
  • Recurrence is Common: 78.3% of patients experience recurrence, but median OS after relapse is 37.1 months
  • LDLT Advantage: 3-year OS 71.4% vs. 51.4% for DDLT
  • HTL + Left-sided Tumors: LT 5-year OS 45.3% vs. PVE+LR 12.5%
