Resources and References

Key Guidelines and Consensus Documents

IHPBA Consensus Guidelines (2021)

Title: Liver transplantation for non-resectable colorectal liver metastases: the International Hepato-Pancreato-Biliary Association consensus guidelines

Authors: Bonney GK, Chew CA, Lodge P, et al.

Journal: The Lancet Gastroenterology & Hepatology, 2021; 6(11):933-946

Significance: Comprehensive consensus document establishing 44 statements on patient selection, biological behavior, graft selection, recipient considerations, and outcomes. Essential reading for all teams considering LT for CRLM.

Expanding Indications in Transplant Oncology (2024)

Title: Liver Transplant for Nonhepatocellular Carcinoma Tumors: A Review of Expanding Indications in Nontraditional Transplant Oncology

Authors: Wehrle CJ, Fares S, Shanmugarajah K, et al.

Journal: Digestive Disease Interventions, 2024

Significance: Comprehensive review of LT for non-HCC tumors including CRLM, cholangiocarcinoma, and neuroendocrine tumors.

Landmark Clinical Trials

TransMet Trial (2024) - First RCT

Title: Liver transplantation plus chemotherapy versus chemotherapy alone in patients with permanently unresectable colorectal liver metastases (TransMet): results from a multicentre, open-label, prospective, randomised controlled trial

Authors: Adam R, Piedvache C, Chiche L, et al.

Journal: The Lancet, 2024; 404(10457):1107-1118

Key Result: 5-year OS 56.6% (LT + Chemo) vs. 12.6% (Chemo alone); HR 0.37 (p=0.0003)

ClinicalTrials.gov: NCT02597348

SECA-I Trial (2013)

Title: Liver transplantation for patients with unresectable liver-only colorectal metastases

Authors: Dueland S, et al.

Key Result: 5-year OS 60% in 21 patients

Significance: Pioneering study that reignited interest in LT for CRLM

SECA-II Trial (2020)

Title: Liver transplantation for unresectable colorectal liver metastases in patients with extended criteria

Authors: Smedman TM, et al.

Key Result: 5-year OS up to 83% with stricter selection criteria

Significance: Demonstrated that refined patient selection improves outcomes

Key Research Papers

Long-Term Survival and Prognostic Factors (2023)

Dueland S, Smedman TM, Syversveen T, et al. Long-Term Survival, Prognostic Factors, and Selection of Patients With Colorectal Cancer for Liver Transplant: A Nonrandomized Controlled Trial. JAMA Surgery, 2023; 158(9):e232932.

Key Findings: 10-year OS 88.9% (Oslo Score 0-1); median OS 60.3 months; identification of prognostic factors

Molecular Prognostic Factors (2025)

Moosavi SH, Kryeziu K, Eilertsen IA, et al. Molecular prognostic factors for liver transplantation of unresectable metastatic colorectal cancer. British Journal of Surgery, 2025; 112(4):znaf072.

Key Findings: RAS/TP53 co-mutations associated with median OS 21 months vs. 60 months; importance of molecular profiling

Comparative Analysis: LT vs. PVE + Resection (2021)

Dueland S, Yaqub S, Syversveen T, et al. Survival Outcomes After Portal Vein Embolization and Liver Resection Compared With Liver Transplant for Patients With Extensive Colorectal Cancer Liver Metastases. JAMA Surgery, 2021; 156(6):550-557.

Key Findings: LT 5-year OS 45.3% vs. PVE+LR 12.5% for HTL with left-sided primary tumors

Current State in the United States (2023)

Sasaki K, Ruffolo LI, Kim MH, et al. The Current State of Liver Transplantation for Colorectal Liver Metastases in the United States: A Call for Standardized Reporting. Annals of Surgical Oncology, 2023; 30(6):3467-3475.

Key Findings: 1-, 2-, 3-year OS 89.0%, 60.4%, 60.4%; 56.5% LDLT use; growing adoption in US centers

Where Are We Now? (2022)

Chávez-Villa M, Ruffolo LI, Tomiyama K, Hernandez-Alejandro R. Where Are We Now With Liver Transplant for Colorectal Metastasis? Current Transplantation Reports, 2022; 9(4):273-282.

Key Findings: Comprehensive review of current evidence, ongoing trials, and institutional experience

Useful Tools and Calculators

Oslo Score Calculator

Use this scoring system to stratify patients:

  • 1 point: Largest tumor size ≥ 5.5 cm
  • 1 point: Progressive disease on chemotherapy
  • 1 point: CEA > 80 µg/L
  • 1 point: Time from primary resection to LT < 2 years

Score 0-2: Favorable; Score 3-4: Unfavorable

Fong Clinical Risk Score (FCRS)

Alternative prognostic tool:

  • 1 point each: Node-positive primary, DFI < 12 mo, >1 tumor, CEA > 200 ng/mL, largest tumor > 5 cm

Score 0-2: Favorable; Score ≥3: Unfavorable

PET-MTV Assessment

Metabolic tumor volume measured on 18F-FDG PET/CT:

  • < 70 cm³: Favorable (5-year OS 63.6%)
  • ≥ 70 cm³: Unfavorable (5-year OS 25%)

Multidisciplinary Team Approach

Successful LT for CRLM requires collaboration among:

  • Hepatobiliary Surgeons: Surgical expertise in liver transplantation
  • Transplant Physicians: Perioperative management and immunosuppression
  • Medical Oncologists: Systemic therapy and response assessment
  • Radiologists: Imaging interpretation and disease burden assessment
  • Pathologists: Tumor characteristics and molecular profiling
  • Colorectal Surgeons: Primary tumor management
  • Interventional Radiologists: Locoregional therapies if needed
